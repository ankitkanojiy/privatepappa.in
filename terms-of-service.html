PrivateVault PRO & PrivateVault App Description

PrivateVault PRO & PrivateVault are offline encrypted vault apps for Android designed to help users store and manage personal files locally on their device. The apps focus on local privacy, file organization, and secure viewing without cloud accounts, internet access, telemetry, or online file viewers.

Both apps utilize industry-standard AES-256 encryption in GCM mode (Galois/Counter Mode) to ensure that imported files cannot be read or misused without proper local authentication. Encryption keys are generated and stored securely on your device using key derivation functions like PBKDF2 or Argon2. This local-only architecture means your files are never transmitted to external servers, protecting them against cloud leaks or unauthorized network access.

Some advanced viewing, scanning, and document features depend on bundled native components such as PDFium and OpenCV.

Limitations

PrivateVault PRO & PrivateVault cannot guarantee that every file type will preview or render perfectly. Unsupported, corrupted, extremely large, unusual, encrypted, or password-protected files may fail to open, preview, or process.

Scan-related features may be imperfect and depend on image quality, lighting, document angle, and supported native components.

Screenshot protection and app obfuscation reduce risk but cannot provide absolute protection against rooted devices, compromised systems, external cameras, screen mirroring bypasses, malware, or advanced reverse engineering.

PrivateVault PRO & PrivateVault do not provide cloud recovery, server-side password reset, or developer recovery access. If a vault password is forgotten or lost, encrypted files may be permanently inaccessible.

Encryption itself does not damage files. The real risk is an interrupted or incomplete write — caused by the phone powering off, storage running out, the app being force-closed, or cleaner apps modifying vault data. PrivateVault uses authenticated encryption, which means even one altered byte will cause the app to block access to that file as a safety feature.

For technical accuracy, Android's built-in Privacy Dashboard and permissions indicators track resource access but do not determine the intent behind data transfers. While Android continues to improve security, local offline encryption remains a critical layer of protection once permissions are granted.
