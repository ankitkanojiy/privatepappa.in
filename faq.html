Frequently Asked Questions

Short, honest answers. If you don’t find yours, write to reply@privatepappa.in.

Does Private Photos require an internet connection?

No. The app does not request the INTERNET permission at all. It is mechanically incapable of opening a network socket. You can verify this in Android Settings › Apps › Private Photos › Permissions.

What happens if I forget my master password?

Your master password is the encryption key. We never store it, and we have no “reset link” to send, because there is no server to send it from.

Without the password, your photos are mathematically unrecoverable. Please choose a password you will remember, and consider writing it on paper kept in a safe place — the same way you would treat a house key.

Where are my photos stored?

Inside the app’s private storage on your device — a sandboxed folder that no other app can read on a non-rooted phone. Each photo is split into encrypted blocks with a unique nonce per file, and the index that maps them back together is itself encrypted.

Can I back up the vault?

Yes. The app can export the entire vault as a single sealed file — still encrypted, still requiring your password to open. You can then copy that file to a USB drive, your computer, or any cloud service. The cloud only sees opaque bytes; it cannot read your photos.

To restore, install the app on the new device, choose Restore from backup, and enter your password.

What image and video formats are supported?

JPEG, PNG, HEIC/HEIF, WebP, GIF and TIFF are fully supported with EXIF preservation. Videos are encrypted as opaque files (you decrypt them to your gallery to play).

Is the app free? Are there ads or trackers?

The core app is free with no ads, no analytics SDKs, no trackers and no in-app purchases at launch. We may add an optional one-time pro upgrade later (e.g. unlimited backup slots), but the core privacy guarantees stay free forever.

How is this different from a hidden folder or file locker?

Most “hidden folder” apps simply rename your files or move them — the bytes are still readable by any file manager. Private Photos encrypts the actual byte stream with AES-256-GCM. Even if someone copies the encrypted files off your phone, they cannot be opened without your password.

Does the app prevent screenshots?

Yes. The vault enables Android’s FLAG_SECURE flag, which blocks screenshots, blocks screen recording on most devices, and hides the app preview in the recent-apps switcher.

Will there be an iOS version?

Yes — we plan to release an iOS version with the same offline guarantees (sandboxed storage, on-device AES-256-GCM, Team ID pinning). Subscribe to updates by emailing hello@privatepappa.in with the subject “iOS waitlist.”

Who is behind this app?

Private Photos is developed by privatepappa.in, an independent team. We are not VC-funded and do not sell user data, because we don’t collect any.

How do I report a bug or request a feature?

Email reply@privatepappa.in. We read every message. Please include screenshot of the bug, your phone model and Android version if reporting a bug.
