About Private Photos

A photo vault built around a single, stubborn idea: your photos belong only to you.

Our Mission

Every cloud-photos app promises privacy and then asks for an account, an email, a phone number, location, contacts, telemetry — because “it makes the product better.” We disagree. Private Photos is built on the opposite principle: if we don’t need it, we won’t collect it. If we can’t protect it perfectly, we won’t store it at all.

The app has no internet permission, talks to no servers, and runs entirely on your device. The only thing leaving your phone is whatever you deliberately export — still encrypted.

How it works

  • You set a master password. Argon2 / PBKDF2 derives a 256-bit key from it. The password is never stored — only a hash that can be verified.
  • Every photo is encrypted byte-by-byte with AES-256-GCM, an authenticated cipher used to protect classified government data.
  • The originals are securely overwritten from your gallery once they’re safely inside the vault.
  • The encrypted index lives in your app’s private storage — no other app on your phone can read it.
  • The screen is hidden from screenshots and recent apps via Android’s FLAG_SECURE.
  • The APK verifies its own signature on every launch. A tampered build wipes itself before you can be tricked.

Kerckhoffs’s principle: a cryptosystem should be secure even if everything about it, except the key, is public. That’s why we openly publish that we use AES-256-GCM — the strength is in your password, not in any secret. Use a strong one.

What we believe

  • Privacy is not a feature. It’s the entire product.
  • Offline is the strongest sandbox. Nothing can leak data that nothing can send.
  • Strong passwords beat clever marketing. We don’t pretend to recover a key we never had.
  • Open about what we do, quiet about what you do. Code paths are documented; your photos aren’t.

The Developer

Private Photos is designed and developed by privatepappa.in — an independent team focused on building tools that respect their users. We are not funded by ads, we don’t sell data, and we don’t have an analytics dashboard. Our “growth metric” is whether the app does what it says.
