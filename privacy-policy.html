Overview

PrivateVault PRO & PrivateVault are designed as offline encrypted vault apps. The apps are intended to protect files locally on your device and do not intentionally collect, sell, transmit, upload, or share your personal vault contents.

Data Collection

In the intended offline production configuration, PrivateVault PRO & PrivateVault do not collect user data through analytics, advertising SDKs, tracking SDKs, cloud sync, or remote servers.

The app does not intentionally collect:

  • Photos, videos, audio, or documents stored in your vault
  • Vault passwords or encryption keys
  • Contacts, messages, call logs, or location
  • Advertising identifiers or behavioral analytics
  • Usage tracking or crash analytics

Permissions and File Access

PrivateVault PRO & PrivateVault request only permissions needed for user-selected app features. Media and file access permissions are used to import, preview, encrypt, decrypt, export, or manage files that you choose.

  • READ_MEDIA_IMAGES may be used for selected image imports and previews.
  • READ_MEDIA_VIDEO may be used for selected video imports and previews.
  • READ_MEDIA_AUDIO may be used for selected audio imports and previews where enabled.
  • Android file picker access may be used so you can choose documents or folders.
  • CAMERA may be requested only when you choose direct camera capture or document scan features.

Core vault operation is designed strictly not to require internet permission. While some apps combine file or media access with internet permissions (which can create a risk of data being transferred outside your device under the guise of syncing or backups), PrivateVault's lack of internet access removes this risk entirely.

How Data Is Used?

Files you select are used locally to provide vault features, including encryption, decryption, local preview, local thumbnails, local search, export, sharing, and organization.

Vault data is not sent to the developer by the app in the intended offline configuration.

Sharing and Disclosure

PrivateVault PRO & PrivateVault do not intentionally share your vault contents with third parties. Files may leave the app only when you explicitly export or share decrypted files using your device's storage picker or Android share sheet.

Security

The app uses strong, industry-standard AES-256 symmetric encryption (with 256-bit keys) applied in secure modes like AES-GCM (Galois/Counter Mode). This provides both confidentiality and integrity protection, allowing the system to detect and reject any unauthorized tampering with encrypted data.

Encryption keys are derived locally using hardened key derivation functions such as PBKDF2 or Argon2, incorporating salting and multiple hashing iterations to resist brute-force and dictionary attacks. All encryption, decryption, and key storage happen entirely on your device.

Other security layers include secure vault sessions, protected previews, screenshot blocking on sensitive screens, and temporary file cleanup. For technical accuracy, while Android provides privacy indicators and dashboards for permissions, they only track when access occurs, not how data is processed afterward. Local, offline encryption remains a critical defense layer once permissions are granted.

Children's Privacy

PrivateVault PRO & PrivateVault are not directed to children under 13 and do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Data Safety Summary

  • Data sold: No
  • Data shared by default: No
  • Data collected by developer servers: No, in the intended offline configuration
  • Cloud sync: No mandatory cloud sync
  • Ads or tracking SDKs: Not intentionally included in the offline vault configuration
